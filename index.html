We believe that energy efficient technology adoption by homeowners has been restricted primarily by ineffective available financing options. Plenty technologies have a positive NPV and more are coming to market. Our new financing model is the only approach that addresses every barrier encountered by individual homeowners and enables them to act as a community to retrofit many existing homes a group achieving sufficient scale to make an impact.

The Milken Institute, McKinsey, and the Rockefeller Foundation have all produced detailed studies between 2009 and 2012 that validate the market size, need, barriers, and financial models. All of the current models are flawed because they try to address 100% of the homeowners in a market. Our solution is targeted at the 25% of homeowners that are lucky enough to live within the boundaries of a community association. Although our solution works equally well for condominiums, we have focused initially on just the subset of 6 million homes in HOAs that were constructed before 1990 and will benefit most from retrofits (an expected 30% energy improvement for a $7,500 to $20,000 retrofit).

Our goal is to make it easy for entire communities to select technology that makes sense for the relocation and the age of the home, finance the deal, and coordinate installation services. We can generate a stream of multi- million dollar transactions.

Problem Being Solved

Our target market are the 6 million single-family detached homes built before 1990 that are within the boundaries of about 67,000 community associations. We can lower the homeowners bill by deploying NPV positive technology today and finance those transactions via assessments. This will allow community-level aggregated transactions while addressing transfer of property and lien security issues.

Customers/market

Today only 200,000 home energy retrofits are occurring annually. The typical HOA has 100 members although some are much larger. We expect the typical retrofit to be $7,500 to $20,000 financed over 5 to 20 years. Using the recent estimate from the Rockefeller Foundation that 61% of homes could benefit at least 30% in efficiency from readily available products, our market is 4 million single family homes worth $40 billion in retrofits. An equal number of condos could benefit too.

Competition

PACE programs that fund retrofits through the issuance of municipal bonds and collect payment through property taxes are gaining traction. Most of the public utilities have some sort of trial program for on-bill or meter-based financing, none of these have scaled. We recently encountered another startup - Effortless Energy, which is trying to apply the shared savings commercial model to homes

Regulatory Considerations

The Obama Administration has pushed weatherization programs through DOE for low income houses targeting entire communities for scale. DOE Building America has also discussed community associations.
